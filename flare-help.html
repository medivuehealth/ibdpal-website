IBD flare help

Content note: Reviewed for patient education accuracy against publicly available guidance from the Crohn's & Colitis Foundation and major IBD education sources. Last reviewed June 2026. Not individual medical advice.

Educational use only. IBDPal does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult your gastroenterologist or IBD care team for personal decisions.

Flares are frightening but manageable with a plan. These guides and articles cover early steps many patients discuss with their IBD team.

Topics: IBD flare, crohn's flare what to do, ulcerative colitis flare, flare diet

When to seek urgent care

Heavy bleeding, severe pain, fever, signs of dehydration, or rigid abdomen need prompt medical evaluation. Use your clinic's on-call instructions.
