Hydration Tips for Crohn’s Disease and Ulcerative Colitis

Posted on May 20, 2026 · Wellness & IBD

Educational use only. This article is general information and not medical advice. If you have signs of severe dehydration or ongoing symptoms, contact your healthcare team.

Why Hydration Is Important

Hydration is often overlooked in IBD management, yet diarrhea, inflammation, and reduced appetite can increase the risk of dehydration. Even mild dehydration may contribute to fatigue, headaches, dizziness, and reduced concentration.

Maintaining adequate fluid intake can support overall wellness and recovery.

Signs of Dehydration

Possible signs include:

  • dry mouth
  • dark urine
  • dizziness
  • fatigue
  • headaches
  • increased thirst

People experiencing persistent symptoms should contact healthcare professionals.

Helpful Hydration Strategies

Drink Small Amounts Consistently

Some people tolerate smaller sips throughout the day better than drinking large amounts at once.

Include Electrolytes

Electrolytes may help replenish minerals lost through diarrhea.

Limit Excess Caffeine

High caffeine intake may worsen dehydration in some individuals.

Monitor Symptoms

Tracking hydration alongside symptoms can help identify patterns between fluid intake and flare severity. Apps like IBDPal can support logging habits and trends to discuss with your care team.

Final Thoughts

Hydration needs vary from person to person. Building consistent hydration habits and monitoring symptoms may help individuals feel more energized and prepared during both flares and remission.

Medical Disclaimer

This article is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult your healthcare provider regarding dietary or medical decisions.
