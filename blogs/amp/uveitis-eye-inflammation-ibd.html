Eye Inflammation and Uveitis in IBD

Posted on July 13, 2026 · Wellness

Content note: Educational content aligned with publicly available patient materials from the Crohn's & Colitis Foundation and other major IBD education sources. IBDPal is not affiliated with or endorsed by the Foundation. Last reviewed June 2026. Not individual medical advice.

Educational use only. IBDPal does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult your gastroenterologist or IBD care team for personal decisions.

IBD inflammation can reach beyond the gut. The eyes are one of the extraintestinal sites patients should take seriously. Redness, pain, blurred vision, or light sensitivity is not something to "wait out" for weeks.

Patterns clinicians talk about

  • Uveitis: inflammation inside the eye that can threaten vision
  • Episcleritis: surface redness that may track with gut flares
  • Dry eye or medication-related irritation

Only an eye exam can sort these out. Do not self-treat with leftover steroid drops.

Seek eye care the same day if

  • Eye pain with light sensitivity
  • Sudden vision change or floating new spots with pain
  • One eye that is red and aching while you feel systemically ill

Tell the ophthalmology team you have IBD and list biologics, steroids, and immunosuppressants. Coordinate with your gastroenterologist if eye disease influences systemic therapy.

How this links to other IBD symptoms

Joint flares and eye flares sometimes travel together. See joint pain and arthritis in IBD and leg and knee pain. Keep a one-line log of eye days next to stool notes.

Questions for your team

  • Do I need a standing ophthalmology relationship on immunosuppression?
  • Could my current IBD medicines help or complicate eye inflammation?
  • What drops or systemic changes are safest for me?
