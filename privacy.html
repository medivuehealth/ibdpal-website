Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

1. Introduction

MediVue operates ibdpal.org (the marketing and education website) and the IBDPal iOS app. This Privacy Policy covers both. Practices differ by product:

  • Website (ibdpal.org): Educational browsing only. No account is required. We do not collect personal health information through the website.
  • IBDPal iOS app: Optional health and lifestyle logging for people who choose to use the app. Encryption, accounts, and health-data protections described below apply to the app, not to simply visiting the website.

2. Website (ibdpal.org)

You can read patient education pages, guides, and articles without creating an account. The website is not a medical record system and is not used to store meal logs, symptoms, medications, or other personal health information.

  • We do not ask website visitors to submit PHI (personal health information) as a condition of browsing.
  • We do not sell website visitor information.
  • If you voluntarily email us (for example support@ibdpal.org) or submit a contact or newsletter form, we use that information only to respond or to send the updates you requested.
  • Statements about end-to-end encryption of health data, HIPAA-oriented app infrastructure, account deletion of health logs, and similar protections apply to the IBDPal iOS app, not to ordinary website browsing.

3. IBDPal iOS App: Information We Collect

The following applies when you download and use the IBDPal iOS application.

3.1 Personal Health Information (app)

  • Medical symptoms and flare tracking data
  • Nutrition and meal logging information
  • Medication schedules and dosages
  • Bowel health records
  • Diagnosis and treatment history you choose to enter

3.2 Account Information (app)

  • Email address and password
  • Profile information (age, gender, location) if you provide it
  • App usage patterns and preferences

3.3 Technical Information (app)

  • Device information and operating system
  • App performance data
  • Crash reports and error logs

4. How We Use App Information

  • Provide personalized nutrition and health insights in the app
  • Generate trend analysis and health reports for you
  • Improve app functionality and user experience
  • Send reminders and notifications you enable
  • Conduct research to improve IBD care (anonymized data only, where permitted)

5. App Data Security

For information stored in the IBDPal iOS app, we implement industry-standard security measures:

  • Encryption for health data in transit and at rest, as implemented in the app and its supporting services
  • Secure cloud storage with controls designed for sensitive health information
  • Regular security reviews and updates
  • Access controls and authentication protocols

These security measures apply to the app experience. They are not a description of how the public education website stores visitor health records, because the website does not collect that type of data.

6. Data Sharing (app)

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal health information from the app. We may share app data only in limited circumstances:

  • With your explicit consent
  • To comply with legal obligations
  • To protect user safety or prevent fraud
  • With healthcare providers (only with your permission)

7. Your Rights (app accounts)

If you have an IBDPal app account, you generally have the right to:

  • Access your personal health information in the app
  • Correct or update your data
  • Delete your account and associated app data
  • Export your health data where the app provides export tools
  • Opt out of data processing for research where that option is offered

8. Children's Privacy

IBDPal is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 through the app. The website is educational content for a general audience and does not target children for data collection.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. For material changes that affect app users, we will notify you through the app or email when appropriate. Website updates may be posted on this page with a revised “Last updated” date.

10. Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us:

  • Email: privacy@ibdpal.org
  • Address: MediVue, North Carolina, USA
